AllVax: Designing a Vaccine System When Equity Is the Constraint

A county health department. An 8-week deadline. A pandemic. I designed the system with equity as the constraint — not the feature. Community co-design with church groups and disability advocates before a line of code was written. 40,000 registrations in 24 hours.

The Problem

A pandemic arrived and Lake County had no infrastructure to register, schedule, and distribute vaccines equitably to a diverse population of hundreds of thousands. Beyond the immediate crisis, the problem was structural: the communities that most needed protection — Latinx residents, Black residents, people with disabilities, those without internet access — were exactly the communities most likely to be failed by a standard registration system built for the majority.

The standard approach would have been to stand up a scheduling portal as fast as possible and fix the equity gaps later. I made a different decision.

The Decision

I designed the system with equity as the design constraint from the beginning — not as a compliance requirement, not as an afterthought, but as the primary architectural forcing function. Before we wrote a line of code, we ran a community co-design process with church groups, Latinx community leaders, and disability advocates.

The questions that process surfaced shaped the system: What happens if someone doesn't have a smartphone? What happens if the registration form only exists in English? What happens if the scheduling system isn't screen-reader compatible? Building it right the first time was harder and slower. But it meant the system actually worked for the people who needed it most.

I also built to scale from day one — knowing that other communities would need this model, and that the architecture needed to support it.

What I Built

AllVax Portal — a comprehensive vaccine orchestration platform on Salesforce Customer 360 for Government. The platform integrated Experience Cloud for the public-facing registration and scheduling interface, Health Cloud for patient and vaccination record management, Marketing Cloud for multilingual outreach, MuleSoft for automated state and federal reporting integration, Tableau for real-time inventory and equity monitoring, and Shield for HIPAA-compliant data protection.

The system was multilingual, ADA-accessible, and screen-reader compatible from launch. It automated real-time reporting to state and federal systems via MuleSoft, eliminating manual data entry and the errors that came with it. Tableau dashboards gave leadership and the public a real-time view of vaccination rates and equity gaps — the same dashboard was viewed 2 million times in the first year.

The Result

  • 40,000 community members registered in the first 24 hours — 200K+ in first weeks, reaching 25% of county population
  • 40% reduction in COVID-19 death disparities between Black, white, and Hispanic residents in one year
  • Lake County was the last county in Illinois to experience its first COVID-19 death
  • 2 million+ views of the COVID data dashboard in one year
  • 250% increase in participatory decision-making among historically underrepresented communities
  • 75 community partners engaged in the community health improvement framework
  • Constellation Research Business Transformation 150 Honoree (2022)

About

Both sides of the table.

Most health IT leaders are one thing. Jefferson is the exception — the operator who became the builder, because he'd been the person who needed what didn't exist.

The Thesis

Jefferson McMillan-Wilhoit is the person who has sat on both sides of the table — and built the table.

Most health IT leaders are one of three things: an executive who understands the business but can't build, a technologist who can build but doesn't understand the institution, or a consultant who understands neither from the inside. Jefferson is none of those.

He was the CIO who had to make the technology decisions that affected 95 people, a $14.5M budget, and an entire county's health outcomes. He knows what it feels like when the system fails at 2am during a mass vaccination event. He knows what it costs — in human terms — when the data isn't right.

And then he went and built the platform from scratch. Not "led the team that built it." Not "oversaw the architecture." He wrote the infrastructure. Seven layers. HIPAA-compliant. Multi-tenant. On AWS. Because he'd been the CIO who needed it and couldn't find it anywhere.

That combination — operator credibility and builder credibility, simultaneously — essentially doesn't exist at his level.

The Through Line

One story, told across 15 years.

Jefferson was trained to see complex systems and find where they fail. That discipline — aviation human factors, applied at the NTSB, refined through years of clinical informatics — is the foundation of everything he builds.

2008 – 2012
Aviation & Human Factors
Graduated Magna Cum Laude from Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University. Principal Investigator, Hypoxia Research Lab — published research, first-named author. Applied human factors expertise at the FAA and NTSB: rigorous, evidence-based investigation of why complex systems fail. This is where the mental model was built.
2012 – 2016
Clinical Informatics & GIS Discovery
Joined Children's National Medical Center in Washington, D.C. as a Clinical Informatics Analyst. Promoted twice. Applied GIS analysis to pediatric injury data and found something no one else had seen: a cluster of burn injuries traced to a single landlord's improperly set water heaters. The data found the children before the system did. That discovery is why Jefferson does what he does. Also received a patent for clinical informatics innovation during this tenure.
2017 – 2018
EHR Integration at Scale
Joined Northwestern University as Manager, Healthcare Analytics. Directed implementation of an enterprise data warehouse unifying EHR data across 4 systems and 21 care locations — one of the most complex multi-site health system integrations in the region. Built an organization-wide reporting portal that gave every level of the organization access to its own data.
2018 – 2022
Public Health CIO — and a Pandemic
Became Director of Health Informatics at Lake County Health Department in 2018, promoted to CIO and CDO in 2019. Oversaw $14.5M in budget, 95 staff, and every technology decision in a complex FQHC and public health enterprise. When COVID-19 arrived, Jefferson designed and built the AllVax vaccine orchestration platform — 40,000 registrations in 24 hours, built in 8 weeks. Lake County was the last county in Illinois to record its first COVID death. National press followed.
2022 – Present
Founder, Cloud Architect, CEO
Co-founded Flourish & Thrive Labs with his wife, Juliana. Personally architected PH360 — a 7-layer, HIPAA-compliant, multi-tenant AI platform on AWS — because he'd been the CIO who needed it. Grew revenue 30× in year one. Currently completing a DrPH at Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health, with a dissertation proposal on the cost-effectiveness of digital disease surveillance systems — submitted February 2026.
How He Works

Values expressed through what gets built.

The Builder

Jefferson built the desk he works at. He built the kitchen table his family has eaten dinner on for eight years — constructed over a holiday weekend. He references Dorothy Sayers: whatever you build should be worth building. That same standard applies to every data system, every platform, every team.

The Prepared One

Often asked how he knows everything he knows, Jefferson's answer is simple: he prepares obsessively. He thinks through every question before a meeting. He always has a Plan A, a Plan B, and a Plan C. He was an expert competitive debater — trained to argue every side of a question until the right answer becomes clear.

The Ethical Operator

His first role out of university exposed him to behavior he couldn't accept. He left. That moment is the root of every ethical commitment he's made since — and the reason F&T Labs was built around values first, revenue second. The 30× growth is a consequence of getting the values right.

The Systems Thinker

From aviation crash analysis to pediatric burn data to mass vaccination logistics, Jefferson has always been where the data meets the human cost. He sees processes and asks how they can be made better. He sees complexity and asks where the single point of failure is. That question — applied rigorously, repeatedly — is the through line.
